I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue Univeristy, advised by [Prof. Kaushik Roy](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=to4P8KgAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate). My research focuses on developing novel algorithms for training-free Neural Architecture Search, model compression and data pruning. My research sits at the intersection of large-scale model optimization and training, geometry and topology. I am driven by the challenge of making artificial intelligence more efficient, accessible, and interpretable with high-performance models.

Research Focus
======
My research focuses on three core pillars of modern Machine Learning Engineering:
1. Model Compression & Efficiency: Developing training-free, zero-shot frameworks like TraceNAS to optimize LLM architecture (depth and width) across models ranging from 2B to 70B+ parameters.
2. Efficient Training via Topology: Developing topologically grounded saliency metrics using topological data analysis and peristence homology for data pruning and lightweight training.
3. Geometric Structured Pruning: Leveraging local spectral isometry and diffusion geometry for multimodal generative model pruning, specifically MMDiTs and Flow Matching models.  
4. Data Explainability & Interpretability: Using Sparse Auto Encoders (SAEs) and mechanistic interpretability to disentangle feature representations and identify memorization patterns within foundation models.

Academic & Professional Journey
======
Prajna G. Malettira is a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University, where she is advised by [Prof. Kaushik Roy](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=to4P8KgAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate) in the [Neuro(nano)electronics Research Lab (NRL)](https://engineering.purdue.edu/NRL). Currently an SRC Research Fellow, her research centers on developing efficient and robust machine learning systems through architectural optimization and geometric analysis. Her work spans several key domains: zero-shot Neural Architecture Search (NAS) for Large Language Model (LLM) pruning, topological data pruning via persistent homology, and efficiency in Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs). Additionally, she is exploring model memorization using Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) and geometry-aware compression for generative models. Her work have been published at venues such as Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR) and the NeurIPS Workshop on Symmetry and Geometry in Neural Representations (NeuREPS).

Prior to pursuing her doctorate, Prajna gained industrial experience in both R&D and operations. As an intern at ABB India, she developed predictive maintenance models using Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). She also served as a project intern at Collins Aerospace, where she designed an operations framework for Cargo Systems to automate work orders and facilitate Just-in-Time process implementation. 

Prajna earned her B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from PES University. During her undergraduate career, she was recognized for her leadership and commitment to social impact as a Millennium Fellow.
